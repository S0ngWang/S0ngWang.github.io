Heartbeats Separating

Submitted 14 Oct 2017.

One of main challenges in heartbeat sensing research is to monitor multiple heartbeats separately, but separating them has received little attention. Even with the presence of multiple people on a bed, as the vibrations caused by multiple heartbeats are often inseparable with regards to time and frequency domain. After carefully conducting a literature review, we have found that a problem similar to this has already been addressed in acoustic sound separation. We believed that by blind source separation, projecting data into a 3D delay-attenuation coordinate system taking into consideration the weighted energy of each frequency component, will enable us to separate sources of heartbeats due to their spatial signatures. We designed a novel algorithm to overcome the complex propagation properties of the bed and mattress. Finally, we proposed a modified model named Degenerate Unmixing Estimation Technique (DUET), which achieved significant improvement in the experimental evaluations, where an average estimation error was below 2 beats per minute.


Separating Heartbeats from Multiple People on One Bed Using Geophones
Zhenhua Jia, Jingxian Wang, Mussab Alaziz, Yanyong Zhang, Richard Howard, Pei Zhang, Chenren Xu
